# Nano Banana Pro 2 — Social Media Visual Storytelling Guide

## Philosophy
Do NOT think like a photographer. Think like a STORYTELLER and COPYWRITER.
Every image must tell a story. The product is part of a moment, not just an object.
Ask yourself: "What emotion does this create? What moment is this?"

## Step 1 — Find The Story Concept
Before writing any prompt, define:
- WHO is in this moment? (family, young couple, friends, solo person)
- WHAT are they doing? (watching movie, having tea, celebrating, relaxing)
- WHERE are they? (cozy home, modern kitchen, outdoor picnic, café)
- WHAT EMOTION does the viewer feel? (warmth, joy, nostalgia, excitement)
- HOW does the product fit naturally into this moment?

## Step 2 — Competitive Analysis Mindset
Look at how top brands create lifestyle content:
- Ülker: Family moments, warm home settings, traditional values
- Nestle: Active lifestyle, modern young people, colorful energy
- Ferrero Rocher: Luxury moments, gifting, premium occasions
- Local brands: Authenticity, cultural connection, familiar settings

The prompt should feel like it belongs among these world-class brands.

## Step 3 — Prompt Structure
Write in this order:

**[SCENE SETTING]** — Describe the environment and moment first
**[PEOPLE/SUBJECTS]** — Who is in the scene (if any), their mood and action  
**[PRODUCT PLACEMENT]** — How the product naturally appears in the scene
**[VISUAL DETAILS]** — Lighting, colors, textures, props
**[COMPOSITION]** — Camera angle, framing, depth of field
**[MOOD/EMOTION]** — The feeling the image evokes
**[TECHNICAL SPECS]** — Format, quality, style

## Step 4 — Copywriting Integration
The slogan must:
- Connect emotionally with the target audience
- Be short (max 6 words in Azerbaijani)
- Create desire or nostalgia or joy
- Feel natural, not forced
Examples of good slogans:
- "Ailənlə hər an xüsusidir"
- "Həzzin ən şirin anı"
- "Sevdiklərinlə daha dadlı"

## Critical Technical Rules
- NO text anywhere in the image
- NO logos or brand marks
- NO watermarks
- Product packaging can appear naturally in scene
- Always specify exact pixel size in prompt
- Use positive language (describe what IS there, not what is NOT)

## Format Compositions
- 1:1 (1080x1080): Balanced scene, product naturally centered or on surface
- 4:5 (1080x1350): Vertical lifestyle scene, product in natural setting
- 9:16 (1080x1920): Full story scene, product as part of the moment

## Lifestyle Scene Examples by Product Type

Product: Wafer package naturally placed on coffee table beside her
Mood: Comfort, relaxation, self-reward
Lighting: Warm lamp light, evening atmosphere

### Tea-time Product:
Scene: Family gathered around tea table, steam rising from cups
Product: Package prominently but naturally on the table
Mood: Warmth, togetherness, tradition
Lighting: Soft golden afternoon light through window

### Chocolate/Candy:
Scene: Friends laughing together, sharing snacks
Product: Being passed between friends, natural interaction
Mood: Joy, friendship, spontaneity
Lighting: Bright, cheerful, natural daylight
